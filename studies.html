The Starter League

Visual Design Coursework over 11 Weeks

Week 1-3    Invite Page

Hackathon Version 1
For the first assignment, I chose to make an invite for a local Hackathon. I thought about main elements I wanted to include such as a map or landscape image to get a feeling of where the event is being held. I also wanted the text to feel game like and chose a sans-serif typeface. With a grid, I started laying out content and tried multiple colors to play off the background image. I emphasized important parts of the text with font weight and bolder colors.

View the Page

Hackathon Version 2
I realized the background image was too distracting and the typeface colors were not working to convey the feeling of a start-up hackathon. I decided to start over without a background image. Red and orange are stronger, bolder colors to use. I also changed typefaces and focused on the placement of words, changing the sizes and spacing. The layout in version 1 had too much space between text so I changed the layout to be centered with the most important information at the top and other relevant details following.

View the Page
Week 4-7   Sales Page

Polaroid Version 1
The next assignment was to sell something you own. The polaroid camera was a perfect idea to play off their brand colors. I created a background pattern with these colors and tried a variety of ways to incorporate it with the header. Then, I made a gallery of images with a hover interaction with jQuery to show a variety of images of the what camera looks like and what kind of images a person can take with it. Then, I added detailed information in a column layout to add the specs and quality of the camera.

View the Page

Polaroid Version 2
I wanted the page to be easier to read, and the layout to have more breathing room within each block of text. I liked the background pattern, but didn't feel like it was balancing the header. I decided to get rid of the main gallery and display the images with a block of text next to it, to make it simpler to read. Then I added a gallery to the footer to show sample images.

View the Page
Week 8-11   Editorial Page

Gallery Version 1
For the editorial page, I wanted an article that allowed me to experiment with breaking font sizes and images. I thought a gallery promo would work well for this. I made the header typeface huge and the secondary header's smaller to contrast it. I used bold background colors and broke the article in three columns for easier reading on the web as well as show more images to see what the show looks like. I hightlighted important text to get a quick understanding of what the article is about.

View the Page

Gallery Version 2
I wanted to break the linear spacing so I overlapped the first three blocks. Next, I wanted the article paragraphs to be displayed at a longer width with quotes or images next to each paragraph. I used different width sizes to make the reading more appealing.
